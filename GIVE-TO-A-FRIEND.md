# DictationMic — quick start for friends

Talk, and it types. Works on your computer and your phone, and your notes
follow you between them.

## On your Windows computer

1. Download **https://dictationmic-sync.web.app/downloads/DictationMic-Windows.zip**
   and extract it anywhere (your Desktop is fine).
2. Double-click **DictationMic.exe**. Windows may show a blue warning
   because the app is new — click **More info → Run anyway**.
3. First run only: the little pill shows a percentage while it fetches the
   speech files (about 480 MB, one time). After that it works fully
   offline — nothing you say ever leaves your computer.
4. Click into any text box, **tap F8** (or click the pill) and just talk.
   Your words appear as you speak. Tap again to stop, or hold F8 to talk
   push-to-talk style.
5. Right-click the pill for options — change the hotkey to any key you
   like, switch to copy-to-clipboard mode, and find your saved notes.

Every dictation is kept automatically as a note you can search later.

## On your phone (Android or iPhone)

1. Open **https://dictationmic-sync.web.app** in your phone's browser.
2. Sign in with your email and a password of your choosing — the account
   creates itself the first time.
3. Android: tap ⋮ → **Add to Home screen**. iPhone: tap Share →
   **Add to Home Screen**. Now it opens like a normal app.
4. Tap the mic button and talk — your words appear on screen as you
   speak. Tap again to finish, then **Save note**. (Android's speech
   service may chime softly now and then while it listens — that's
   normal.)
5. Prefer typing or pasting? Tap **+ New** at the top of your notes and
   type straight in — it saves as you go.

## Linking computer and phone

Right-click the pill → **Set up phone sync…** → enter the **same email
and password** you used on the phone. From then on, anything you dictate
on either one shows up on both within seconds — edits, renames and
deletes too. No signal? Notes queue up and sync when you're back online.

Your notes are private to your account — nobody else (including Steve)
can read them.
